KnowledgeOps Demo Policy: Heat Safety Operations

Document owner: Operations Risk Office
Version: 2026.04
Intended tenant: tenant-acme

1. Scope

This policy applies to field inspection, warehouse loading, outdoor maintenance,
and any work pattern where employees may remain in high-temperature conditions
for more than 30 minutes.

2. Risk Classification

Heat exposure risk must be classified by temperature, exposure duration, labor
intensity, ventilation, hydration availability, and protective equipment.
High-risk roles include outdoor inspection, warehouse loading, delivery staging,
and maintenance work without cooling access.

3. Control Requirements

Teams must schedule hydration breaks, rotate workers during peak heat periods,
provide shaded or cooled recovery areas, and record abnormal symptoms through
the incident reporting process. Supervisors must review high-risk work plans
before the shift starts.

4. Emergency Response

If a worker shows dizziness, confusion, fainting, abnormal sweating, or suspected
heat stroke symptoms, the supervisor must stop the task, move the worker to a
cool area, start first aid, and escalate according to the operations runbook.
